India's Independence Day

Date: August 15 (Annual Celebration)

About Independence Day

Independence Day of India is celebrated every year on 15th August to mark the nation's freedom from British rule in 1947. On this day, the Indian Independence Act 1947 came into effect, transferring legislative sovereignty from the United Kingdom to the Indian Constituent Assembly.

Historical Significance

India attained independence after a sustained nonviolent resistance movement led chiefly by Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress. The partition of British India into India and Pakistan also coincided with independence, accompanied by significant challenges and upheaval. The first Independence Day flag hoisting was done by Jawaharlal Nehru at the Red Fort in Delhi on August 15, 1947.

Celebrations

Independence Day is a national holiday celebrated throughout India with flag hoisting ceremonies, parades, cultural events, and patriotic programs. The Prime Minister hoists the national flag at the Red Fort in Delhi and delivers a nationally televised speech. Other states observe their own celebrations with local governors or chief ministers raising the flag. Traditional activities include singing the national anthem and kite flying.

2025 Celebration

In 2025, India will observe its 79th Independence Day, continuing the legacy of patriotism and unity among its citizens. The President of India addresses the nation on the eve of August 15, followed by nationwide festivities.
